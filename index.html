HOW TRANSIT WORKS

🚄

SHINKANSEN (BULLET TRAIN)

Tokyo → Odawara: Tokaido Shinkansen Kodama/Hikari from Tokyo Station, ~35 min.
Odawara → Shin-Osaka: Hikari, ~2h. Reserve right-side window for Fuji views.
Nagoya → Tokyo: Hikari, ~1h 40m.
Use Hikari or Kodama only — Nozomi requires a surcharge with JR Pass.

ACTIVATE 7-DAY JR PASS ON MAY 28
🚇

TOKYO LOCAL TRANSIT

Buy a Suica or PASMO IC card at the airport — tap in, tap out for everything.
JR Yamanote Line loops the city: Shibuya → Shinjuku → Ikebukuro → Ueno → Tokyo → Shinagawa.
Tokyo Metro (Ginza, Hanzomon, Chiyoda lines) covers everywhere else. NOT covered by JR Pass.
Trains run 5am–midnight. Last train ~11:45pm.

SUICA/PASMO FOR ALL LOCAL RIDES
🗺️

OSAKA & KANSAI TRANSIT

Osaka → Kyoto: JR Special Rapid from Osaka Station, ~30 min (JR Pass).
Osaka → Kobe: JR Tokaido-Sanyo Line from Osaka Station to Sannomiya, ~20 min (JR Pass).
Osaka → Nara: JR Yamatoji Rapid from Osaka Station to JR Nara, ~45 min (JR Pass).
Local Osaka: Midosuji Line subway: Umeda ↔ Namba ~10 min. Use IC card.

IC CARD WORKS IN KANSAI TOO

🧳 LUGGAGE FORWARDING (OPTIONAL — STILL DEBATING)

If you decide to ship bags instead of hauling them on the Shinkansen, you'd only need to do this once — sending bags from Tokyo to Osaka. You keep your backpacks and travel light for 2 days.

01
Drop off: May 27 morning (DisneySea day), at your Shibuya hotel front desk before you leave for Disney. Ask front desk to ship via Yamato Transport (クロネコヤマト / Kuroneko). Fill out the form they give you (staff will help). Write your Osaka hotel's full name and address, plus your name. Pay ~¥2,000–3,000 per bag at the desk.
02
Bags arrive: May 29 at your Osaka hotel front desk. Yamato delivers next-day or day-after within Honshu. Your bags will be waiting when you check in after the Shinkansen from Hakone.
03
Return trip: Don't ship. Osaka → Nagakute → Tokyo, you're on the Shinkansen and can bring bags onboard. Use coin lockers (¥400–700) at major stations if you need to stash bags during a day trip (e.g., Nara).

If you DON'T ship: Totally fine. Shinkansen has overhead racks and behind-seat luggage areas. If any bag exceeds 160cm total (L+W+H), reserve a free oversized luggage seat when booking your Shinkansen. Coin lockers at Tokyo Station, Shin-Osaka, and Nagoya handle day-trip bag storage.

KNOW BEFORE YOU GO

SURVIVAL GUIDE

💴 MONEY

Carry ¥15,000–25,000 cash daily (~$100–170). 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards. IC cards work on trains, buses, and konbini. Japan is more card-friendly now but small spots are still cash-only.

🚶 WALKING

15,000–25,000 steps per day. Bring comfortable shoes. Schedule recovery mornings. Japan involves WAY more walking than expected.

📱 NAVIGATION

Google Maps is king for transit. Download offline maps of Tokyo and Osaka. Get a Japan eSIM or pocket WiFi at the airport.

🍜 FOOD RULES

Book ONE high-end omakase early. Convenience stores are incredible. Don't overplan meals — best food is random discoveries.

🚃 JR PASS STRATEGY

Activate 7-day JR Pass on May 28 (Hakone day). Expires Jun 3 — covers Hakone, Shinkansen to Osaka, all Kansai JR lines, Nara, Kobe, and Shinkansen Nagoya → Tokyo. Use IC cards for all local Tokyo transit.

🏨 LODGING

4 hotels total: Tokyo → Ryokan → Osaka → Nagakute → Tokyo. All booked. Great setup — minimal hotel moves.

🌙 NIGHTLIFE

Tokyo: Shibuya > Shinjuku > Roppongi. Golden Gai, Omoide Yokocho, SG Club. Osaka: Namba/Dotonbori, Ura-Namba. Avoid business districts after 8pm.

🎌 ETIQUETTE

No eating while walking. No talking on trains. Stand right, walk left (Tokyo escalators). Don't tip. Learn: arigatou, sumimasen, onegaishimasu.

⚠️ AVOID

Moving hotels too often. Overpacking days. Ignoring recovery mornings. Trying to see ALL of Japan. Not booking reservations early enough.
